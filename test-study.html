Quadrants

The coordinate plane is divided into four sections called quadrants. The quadrants are labeled I, II, III, and IV, starting from the top right and moving counterclockwise. Each quadrant has a unique combination of positive and negative values for x and y coordinates.

Transformations

Transformations involve changing the position of a shape on the coordinate plane. Types of transformations include translations, rotations, reflections, and dilations.

Reflections

Reflections flip a shape across a line, such as the x-axis or y-axis.

Rotations

Rotations turn a shape around a fixed point, typically the origin. Rotations are measured in degrees (90°, 180°, etc.).

Dilations

Dilations resize a shape based on a scale factor. A scale factor greater than 1 enlarges the shape, while a scale factor less than 1 reduces it.
